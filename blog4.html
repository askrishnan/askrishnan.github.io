Sisters Unchained

Watching the Sisters Unchained video drove home how little support there is for the relatives of people who are incarcerated. Many of the interviews referenced how rarely the girls see their relatives who are incarcerated, to the point where Loyalty and Unity barely knew who their dad was when he got out of prison. Many of the girls are raised by other members of their families. Sometimes this requires them to uproot their entire lives to move with other parts of the family. Kyndia mentions having to grow up by herself, living with her grandmother, and having to figure out the parts of her schooling on her own. Several of the girls were very close to their incarcerated family members, and going from always being with them to never seeing them seems like an impossibly difficult transition.

Major takeaways

  • The girls rarely get to see their incarcerated relatives (if their relative was taken to prison when they were very young, they generally don't have a strong relationship with them; it is more of a story they are told)
  • Their entire lives are uprooted if a primary member of their support system is incarcerated.
  • If they do not have a close relationship/resources in the new living situation, the girls have to figure out a lot on their own.

HSTF

The youth notes from the HSTF made it clear that many of the young people are interested in creating an uncensored space just for them. They are proud of the Latinx representation in the area, but hesitant about the potential gentrification that is encroaching on the Latin Quarter. The young people love the events held on the street, and would like more block parties, etc. I think that these events help condense many of the good vibes of the Latin Quarter into one fun event for people to attend. They want to create a larger sense of community by sprucing up HSTF and making it brigher. The current map of the Latin Quarter conveys a lot of valueable information, but it isn't really presented in a tantalizing way.
